About
#HairStory is an open space where hair becomes a form of storytelling. More than appearance, hair can hold memories of girlhood and motherhood, express femininity or masculinity, and mark moments of change, loss, or love.
01.
Start with an image of the person whose hair the story is about Hair should be the focus. It can be a portrait, a close-up, or just the strands.
02.
Connect that image to a story What does the hair say about your life, culture, identity, or experience? When did your hair mean something more than just hair?
03.
Tell it your way Document your story in any form of media: writings, books, videos, audio, posters. Tag #HairStory when you publish it to any platforms
#HairStory
The story of #HairStory started when I came back to Vietnam after two years abroad. My mom picked me up at the airport, and her hair had turned almost completely white. It surprised me because the last time I saw her, her hair was still long and black. I found myself avoiding looking at it, as if not seeing it could slow down time. But I realized it had already passed—quietly, in her hair. White strands became a marker of memory and change. I also noticed something in myself: whenever life feels overwhelming, I cut my hair, especially my bangs.
